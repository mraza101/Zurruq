Compliance & Standards

Traceability you can stake your brand on.

From the moment our cocoa and coffee leaves the farm in Ghana to the day it arrives at your facility, every step is documented, tested, and verified.

🔍

Intake Inspection

Every container arriving in Melbourne undergoes a rigorous physical and documentation check before being cleared for inventory.

Metric: 100% of lots verified against origin COA before warehousing.

📦

Lot-Coded Inventory

Our warehouse management system tracks every bag by its unique origin lot code, ensuring strict First-In-First-Out (FIFO) rotation.

Metric: Zero inventory discrepancies in the last 24 months.

🌡️

Storage Conditions

Our Tullamarine facility is temperature and humidity monitored 24/7 to prevent moisture ingress and preserve product integrity.

Metric: Maintained at <60% RH and 15-20°C year-round.

⏱️

Recall Readiness

We conduct biannual mock recalls to ensure our systems can instantly trace any lot from our warehouse to your delivery dock.

Metric: Full lot trace completed within 2 hours.

The Journey

Documented at every step

1

Sourcing & Processing (Ghana)

Cocoa and coffee are sourced from verified cooperatives and processed in licensed facilities.

Documentation: COCOBOD Licence Number, Farm Cooperative ID, Initial Batch Code.
2

Quality Control & Testing

Before export, every lot is tested by an accredited laboratory for physical, chemical, and microbiological parameters.

Documentation: Certificate of Analysis (COA) per lot, Testing Laboratory Name, Date of Test.
3

Export & Sea Freight

Products are loaded into food-grade, temperature-monitored containers at Tema Port and sealed for the journey to Australia.

Documentation: Bill of Lading, Phytosanitary Certificate, Container Seal Number.
4

Melbourne Distribution

Upon arrival, goods are inspected, logged into our inventory system by lot code, and stored in our climate-controlled warehouse.

Documentation: Intake Inspection Report, Delivery Docket with Lot Code, Invoice.
